# Corelith Logo Design Brief
## For AI Image Generation (ChatGPT Images 2 / DALL-E 3 / Midjourney)

**Company:** Corelith (by Pagka)  
**Product:** Huchu ERP — Business management software for Zimbabwe SMBs  
**Market:** Zimbabwe, Southern Africa, emerging markets  
**Target Audience:** Business owners, operations managers, accountants (25-55, practical, tech-cautious)  
**Brand Personality:** Reliable, modern, African-rooted, no-nonsense, warm but precise  
**Budget Tier:** $30,000 quality aesthetic on a startup budget  

---

## 1. DESIGN STRATEGY

### Core Concept
Corelith = "Core" + "Lithos" (stone/rock in Greek). The brand is about being the **solid foundation** underneath chaotic business operations. The logo should feel like something you can trust to hold your business together.

### The Duality
The logo must balance two energies:
- **Stability** — Stone, structure, foundation, reliability
- **Motion** — Growth, progress, upward trajectory, digital transformation

### Emotional Goal
When someone sees this logo, they should feel:
> *"This company understands my business. They're not trying to be Silicon Valley. They're trying to be useful."*

---

## 2. VISUAL DIRECTION

### Aesthetic References
Study these logos for the FEEL, not to copy:
1. **Linear** — Clean geometric mark, confident minimalism
2. **Notion** — Simple geometric shape, instantly recognizable
3. **Figma** — Warm modernity, approachable precision
4. **Attio** — Confident blue, no decoration, pure function
5. **Vercel** — Abstract triangle, bold and memorable
6. **Framer** — Playful but structured
7. **Basecamp** — Simple mark + confident wordmark

### Style Notes
- **Minimalist** — Not ornate. Not decorative. Every element earns its place.
- **Geometric** — Clean shapes, straight lines, perfect curves. No organic blobs.
- **Bold weight** — Should work at 16px (favicon) and 16ft (billboard).
- **Warm undertone** — Not cold corporate blue. Something with life in it.

---

## 3. COLOR PALETTE

### Primary: Corelith Blue
- **Hex:** `#2563EB` (classic strong blue)
- **RGB:** 37, 99, 235
- **Why:** Conveys trust, technology, clarity. Used by Attio, Stripe, Intercom — proven SaaS blue.

### Secondary: Warm Stone
- **Hex:** `#F5F0EB` (warm off-white)
- **RGB:** 245, 240, 235
- **Why:** Softens the blue. Gives it warmth. References "lithos" (stone).

### Accent: African Earth
- **Hex:** `#D4946A` (terracotta/sand)
- **RGB:** 212, 148, 106
- **Why:** Nods to Zimbabwe/African origin without being cliche. Warm and grounding.

### Dark Mode: Deep Core
- **Hex:** `#0F0F0F` (near-black)
- **RGB:** 15, 15, 15
- **Why:** For dark backgrounds. Not pure black — has depth.

### Usage Rules
- Logo mark: Always Corelith Blue or White
- Background: Warm Stone (light), Deep Core (dark)
- Accent (Earth): Use sparingly — for badges, highlights, not the logo itself

---

## 4. THE MARK (Symbol)

### Concept Directions to Explore

#### Direction A: The Keystone
A keystone is the central stone in an arch that holds everything together. It references "lithos" and communicates that Corelith is the piece that makes the whole business structure work.
- Shape: Trapezoid or angular shape with a slight curve
- Feels: Architectural, structural, essential

#### Direction B: The Core Crystal
A faceted geometric shape (hexagon or octagon) that suggests a core/nucleus. References both "core" and the idea of something solid and multifaceted.
- Shape: Hexagon with internal facets or planes
- Feels: Tech-forward, solid, multifaceted solution

#### Direction C: The Ascending Block
A square or rectangle with an upward-pointing element inside. Suggests building up, growth, and solid foundations.
- Shape: Square with an upward triangle/arrow formed from negative space
- Feels: Growth, construction, upward momentum

#### Direction D: The Intersection
Two geometric forms intersecting to create a new shape. Represents how Corelith connects different parts of a business.
- Shape: Two overlapping rectangles or a plus sign with dimensionality
- Feels: Connection, integration, unified system

### Mark Requirements
- Must work as a standalone favicon (32x32)
- Must work in monochrome (single color)
- Must work on both light and dark backgrounds
- Should NOT be literal (no rocks, no computers, no African maps)

---

## 5. THE WORDMARK

### Typography Direction
- **Style:** Modern geometric sans-serif
- **Weight:** Semibold (600) or Bold (700)
- **Case:** Lowercase or Title Case — NOT ALL CAPS
- **Feel:** Confident, friendly, tech-forward

### Font References (for the feel)
- **Inter** — Clean, modern, already used in the product
- **Plus Jakarta Sans** — Warm geometric, friendly
- **Geist** — Sharp, contemporary
- **Manrope** — Balanced, approachable

### Wordmark Rules
- Letter-spacing: Slightly tight (-0.02em) for cohesion
- The "C" should feel distinctive — maybe a unique cut or angle
- "corelith" should feel like one solid word, not fragmented
- Consider a ligature or subtle connection between "e" and "l"

---

## 6. LOCKUP OPTIONS

### A. Horizontal (Primary)
```
[MARK]  corelith
```
- Mark on the left
- Wordmark on the right
- Gap: ~0.5x mark width
- Use for: Website header, business cards, presentations

### B. Stacked (Secondary)
```
   [MARK]
  corelith
```
- Mark centered above
- Wordmark below
- Use for: App icon, social media avatars, square spaces

### C. Mark Only (Favicon/App Icon)
```
[MARK]
```
- Just the geometric symbol
- Use for: Browser favicon, app icon, small badges

### D. Full Lockup (Formal)
```
   [MARK]  corelith
           by Pagka
```
- "by Pagka" in smaller, lighter weight below
- Use for: Legal docs, formal presentations, investor materials

## 10. ABSOLUTE NO-GOS

These will be rejected immediately:

- **No literal rocks/stones** — The "lith" is conceptual, not visual
- **No African cliches** — No maps of Africa, no tribal patterns, no animals
- **No gradient meshes** — Flat colors only, max 2 colors per lockup
- **No drop shadows** — Flat design only
- **No serif fonts** — Must feel modern and digital
- **No all-caps wordmark** — Lowercase or Title Case only
- **No more than 2 colors** in any single lockup
- **No decorative swooshes** — No swooshes, no swirls, no abstract waves
- **No globe/earth icons** — Not a global company (yet)
- **No puzzle pieces** — Too literal for "integrated system"
