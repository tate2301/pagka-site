# Competitive Brand Audit

## Attio
**Mark:** Custom geometric "A" constructed from a rounded rectangle with a diagonal cut.
**What works:** The mark is both a letter AND a shape. It reads instantly but rewards closer inspection. The proportions feel intentional — not random. Warm cream background makes it feel human.
**Technique:** Single shape with a subtraction. Bold weight. Rounded corners (~20% of side length).

## Linear
**Mark:** Lightning bolt "L" — two angled strokes forming a sharp corner.
**What works:** Extreme minimalism. Every pixel is considered. The angle creates forward momentum. The mark works at 16×16 without losing identity.
**Technique:** Two strokes with specific angle (roughly 30° and 60°). Rounded caps soften the aggression.

## Outseta
**Mark:** Custom wordmark with a distinctive "O" and warm gradient.
**What works:** The wordmark IS the logo. No separate icon. This forces the name to be memorable. The gradient adds depth without complexity.
**Technique:** Custom letterforms with personality. Warm gradient (coral to peach).

## Mailchimp (Current)
**Mark:** Freddie the monkey — hand-illustrated mascot.
**What works:** Instant memorability. Human personality. No other tech company has a mascot this friendly.
**Technique:** Illustration-based. Not replicable for Corelith's category.

## Mailchimp (Rebrand — 2018)
**Mark:** Abstract "M" formed by two overlapping arcs.
**What works:** The cursive hand-drawn quality makes a giant corporation feel approachable. The yellow is optimistic.
**Technique:** Hand-lettered feel. Single color. The imperfection is intentional.

## Notion
**Mark:** "N" formed by a single continuous line with a break.
**What works:** The break creates negative space that feels like a door or portal. Simple but distinctive.
**Technique:** Single stroke. Specific corner radius. Black and white.

## Figma
**Mark:** Five circles arranged in a "F" pattern.
**What works:** Literal construction from primitives (circles). This mirrors how designers use Figma. Meta.
**Technique:** Perfect circles, perfect spacing. Mathematical precision.

## Vercel
**Mark:** Single upward-pointing triangle (delta).
**What works:** One shape. Perfect. The negative space creates a "V". Extreme minimalism.
**Technique:** Equilateral triangle, rotated. No decoration.

---

## Patterns Across the Best Logos

1. **Single shape dominance** — The best marks are one strong shape, not compositions of many elements.
2. **Hidden meaning** — Linear's L is also a lightning bolt. Attio's A is also a container. The mark rewards attention.
3. **Perfect geometry** — Even "organic" logos (Mailchimp) have mathematical precision underneath.
4. **One color or gradient** — Never more than two colors in the mark itself.
5. **Scales to 16px** — If it doesn't work tiny, it's not done.

## SalesIntellect (Zimbabwe)
**Category:** POS/Retail checkout
**Mark:** Unknown
**What they do:** Point of sale, checkout, basic sales tracking
**Their weakness:** POS-only. No stock management. No multi-site. No operations beyond the register.
**How we beat them:** "SalesIntellect handles your checkout. Corelith handles your entire business — stock, assets, compliance, payroll, and sales."

## QuickBooks (International, limited Zim use)
**Category:** Accounting
**Mark:** Green "QB" wordmark
**What they do:** Accounting, invoicing, tax
**Their weakness:** Generic accounting tool. Not built for Zimbabwe load shedding. No mobile operations. No multi-site stock. Not industry-specific.
**How we beat them:** "QuickBooks counts your money. Corelith runs your business. Stock, sales, maintenance, compliance — all on your phone."

Corelith needs:
- A mark that's **one strong shape** (following the pattern)
- **Hidden meaning** — something that reads differently on second look
- **Mathematical precision** — not arbitrary proportions
- **Warmth + solidity** — the tension between "friendly" and "rock-solid"
- **Scalability** — must work at favicon size
