The Problem

The thrift margin mystery.

When you can't trace a bale's cost through to its final sale, you're flying blind on your most important metric: profit.

Common thrift headaches:

  • Bale intake and grading records are hard to trace back to original cost and weight — you bought a bale for $200, but what did each lot cost you?
  • Lot inventory drifts when sales and stock are not tied to bale source — the notebook says one thing, the shelf says another
  • Margins are unclear when landed costs are not connected to final resale price — you think you're making money, but are you?
