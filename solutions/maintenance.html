The Problem

The reactive maintenance trap.

When maintenance is reactive, every repair is an emergency. You lose production time, overtime pay, and peace of mind.

Common maintenance headaches:

  • ✕ Equipment records scattered across logs and spreadsheets — when a machine breaks, you don't know when it was last serviced
  • ✕ Breakdowns are reactive because preventive schedules are hard to track and enforce
  • ✕ Managers can't see which assets cost the most in downtime and repairs — no data for repair vs. replace decisions
